About

Bridging simulation fidelity and materials physics.

CFD engineer turned materials scientist. Currently at TUM, open to Hiwi and working-student roles in Munich.

Photo

I'm an M.Sc. Materials Science student at Technische Universität München with 4+ years of gas-turbine development experience at Honeywell Technology Solutions. My work there spanned conjugate-heat-transfer analysis, transient FOD studies, and full turbomachinery stage aerodynamic development — alongside the Python and VBA tooling that turned raw solver output into decision-ready dashboards.

Those automation pipelines shaved 26% off analysis cycle time and delivered $33K in annual engineering savings. The experience taught me that a CFD result isn't actually useful until it's in the hands of the engineer making the design call, and that the "last mile" of post-processing is often where real leverage hides.

At TUM I'm deepening the physics side: thermomechanical characterisation on the Gleeble 3800, thin-film spray pyrolysis for solid-state electrolytes, SEM-based microstructural analysis, and hot-wire anemometry in the wind tunnel. The goal is to connect high-fidelity simulation with materials behaviour for next-generation propulsion and energy systems.

Outside of engineering I'm learning German (B1, climbing toward B2), and spending weekends in the Alps whenever the weather cooperates.
